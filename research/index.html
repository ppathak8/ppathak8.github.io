My primary research interest is Astronomical instrumentation with emphasis on exoplanet science.

Currently, I am involved in simulating the performance of the High-contrast Imaging (HCI) modes of the METIS instrument. In the framework of METIS I co-developed a general purpose HCI simulator called HEEPS (High-contrast End-to-End Performance Simulator).

Previously, I also worked on one of the modules of SCExAO instrument, which is a high-contrast imaging instrument for direct detection of exoplanets at Subaru telescope. For my PhD thesis, I worked on active on-sky closed-loop correction of residual atmospheric dispersion for high contrast coronagraphy.
